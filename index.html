Restoring Ecosystems & Empowering Communities

Kerio Valley is a vital but fragile landscape in Kenya where people, nature, and livelihoods are closely connected, yet years of deforestation, unsustainable farming, recurrent fires, and climate stress have led to degraded soils, drying rivers, biodiversity loss, and rising poverty. Restoring these landscapes is transforming the valley through community-led agroforestry, reforestation, soil and water conservation, and climate-resilient farming that rebuilds ecosystems while improving household incomes. These efforts protect water catchments, reduce landslides and flood risks, enhance food security, and create sustainable livelihoods, especially for women and youth. By restoring nature while strengthening local economies, Kerio Valley directly advances the Sustainable Development Goals, including SDG 1 on No Poverty, SDG 2 on Zero Hunger, SDG 6 on Clean Water, SDG 13 on Climate Action, and SDG 15 on Life on Land, offering donors a high-impact opportunity to deliver lasting environmental recovery and human well-being through locally owned, scalable solutions.

Our Impact

Improved Food Security

Helping communities achieve stable and sufficient food production.


Food security in Kerio Valley is closely linked to the health of the landscape, yet land degradation, erratic rainfall, and reliance on low-value, climate-sensitive crops have left many households vulnerable to hunger and unstable incomes. Community-led restoration and climate-smart agriculture are strengthening food systems by improving soil fertility, conserving water, and diversifying production through agroforestry, resilient crops, and sustainable livestock practices. These approaches increase yields, extend growing seasons, and create year-round income while reducing the risk of crop failure during droughts or floods. By restoring productive land and empowering farmers with sustainable practices, Kerio Valley is advancing SDG 2 on Zero Hunger, SDG 1 on No Poverty, SDG 12 on Responsible Consumption and Production, and SDG 13 on Climate Action, offering donors a practical, high-impact pathway to build resilient food systems that nourish communities today and safeguard livelihoods for future generations.

Restored Landscapes

Reclaiming degraded lands and bringing back native flora and fauna.

Empowered Communities

Building local capacity for long-term resilience and self-reliance.


Empowered communities are at the heart of sustainable change in Kerio Valley, where local people are moving from vulnerability to leadership in managing their natural resources and livelihoods. Through inclusive participation, skills development, and support to community groups, women, youth, and smallholder farmers are strengthening local governance, adopting climate-resilient practices, and driving landscape restoration and food security initiatives. This community-led approach builds ownership, accountability, and long-term sustainability while creating income opportunities and improving access to essential services. By placing communities at the center of decision-making and action, Kerio Valley advances SDG 5 on Gender Equality, SDG 8 on Decent Work, SDG 10 on Reduced Inequalities, SDG 11 on Sustainable Communities, and SDG 16 on Strong Institutions, offering donors a trusted, scalable model where empowered people deliver lasting social, economic, and environmental impact.

Increased Incomes

Boosting household earnings through sustainable enterprises.


In Kerio Valley, increased income and market access go hand in hand as communities shift from low-return, climate-vulnerable farming to more sustainable and profitable livelihoods. By organizing farmers into cooperatives, communities strengthen collective action, adopt climate-resilient practices, and gain access to training, inputs, and financial services, while linkages to local and regional markets ensure reliable sales and fair prices for high-value crops like coffee, fruit trees, and sustainable livestock. This integrated approach not only boosts household incomes year-round but also reduces poverty, strengthens resilience to climate shocks, and allows families to invest in food, education, and health. By connecting restored landscapes to thriving markets, Kerio Valley advances SDG 1 on No Poverty, SDG 2 on Zero Hunger, SDG 8 on Decent Work and Economic Growth, SDG 9 on Industry and Innovation, and SDG 12 on Responsible Consumption and Production, offering donors a high-impact opportunity to create sustainable, market-driven livelihoods while improving environmental health.
