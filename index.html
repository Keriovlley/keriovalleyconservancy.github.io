About Us

Kerio Valley Conservancy (KVC) is a community-based organization addressing poverty, climate vulnerability, and environmental degradation in the Kerio Valley. Despite its rich ecological and agricultural potential, the region experiences high poverty, land degradation, erratic rainfall, and limited livelihood options.

Most households depend on maize farming and livestock rearing, which are increasingly unsustainable. KVC promotes an integrated, climate-smart model combining agroforestry, sustainable agriculture, and community-led extension systems to restore degraded landscapes while improving incomes and food security.

What We Do

  • Agroforestry & Climate-Smart Livelihoods: Coffee-based agroforestry restores degraded land, conserves soil, enhances biodiversity, buffers microclimates, and generates high-value incomes.
  • Community Nursery Establishment: Farmers are organized into clusters with community-owned coffee and tree nurseries providing affordable, high-quality seedlings.
  • Farmer Extension & Knowledge Transfer: Local farmers are trained as Community Extension Champions to deliver peer-to-peer technical support on crop planting, soil conservation, and climate-smart farming.
  • Market & Cooperative Linkages: KVC connects farmers to cooperatives and reliable buyers to strengthen value chain participation and improve income.
  • Landscape Restoration & Disaster Risk Reduction: Integrated soil and water conservation measures along the 57km hanging Kerio Valley reduce landslide risks and restore ecosystems.

Expected Impact

The Integrated Community Development Agroforestation Project (ICDAP) is projected to double participating households' income within two years while restoring soil, sequestering carbon, and improving biodiversity.
